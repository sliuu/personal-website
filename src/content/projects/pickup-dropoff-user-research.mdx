## Project Brief

Waymo is a self-driving ride-sharing service, at the time operating to the public in San Francisco and Phoenix. User survey data from in-app feedback had shown that poor pickup and dropoff locations often led to high user dissatisfaction. We knew that users had issues with our pickups and dropoffs, but we did not know necessarily why.

The User Research team at Waymo was asked to look into this, and understand what was going wrong.

## Research

We conducted a Diary Study, where users were asked to complete surveys about the pickups and dropoffs of all of their Waymo rides over a 4-week period. This allowed us to get substantive quantitative data for analysis. 

<figure>
<img src="/projects/pickup-dropoff-survey.png" alt="Image of Waymo car view on a road" class="hero"/>
<figcaption> A sample of the questions we asked users </figcaption>
</figure>

We had 100 participants in total, split evenly between San Francisco and Phoenix, between new and existing riders, and with good coverage over general demographic buckets, including age, race/ethnicity, and disabilities.

## Impact
After presenting to cross-functional stakeholders, we assembled a group of designers, engineers, researchers, and PMs to go over potential improvements. One of the main takeaways from these collaboration sessions was the need for more coordination between onboard and offboard systems, and for better communication with the user to improve on certainty expectations.

In addition to influencing product roadmaps and priorities, the data from this study and the user-provided “better spots” from the survey were then used by engineering teams to improve the model’s choice of pickup and dropoff location going forward.
